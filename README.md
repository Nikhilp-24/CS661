Knowledge and Wealth Paradox

Academic research serves as a fundamental driver of global innovation, human capital development, and economic growth.
However, the global scientific landscape exhibits deep systemic disparities. While total research spending is heavily
concentrated within high-income nations, different countries extract highly variable scientific value from their investments.
Our project aims to develop a web-based, interactive visual analytics application that dissects the global academic
ecosystem. By mapping macroeconomic parameters against global publishing indexes and institutional distributions,
this system will investigate how national wealth converts into scientific excellence, isolate structural anomalies, evaluate
the impact of local purchasing power, and In addition, the project provides a focused analysis of India’s rapidly growing higher education and research
sector. Through intuitive visualizations, users can track how different countries have contributed to the global academic
landscape over time and observe changes in both the quantity and quality of research at the global level as well as within
India. The goal is to make complex research trends accessible, enabling deeper insights into the factors that influence
scientific progress worldwide.
